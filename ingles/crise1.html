  1. Crise of Public Security in Espírito Santo

On February 3, 2017, in the morning, relatives of the police protested in front of the military battalion of the Feu Rosa, Serra neighborhood, preventing the departure of vehicles. These occurrences happened also in regions of Jaaraípe, New Almeida, Manguinhos and Vila Nova de Colares where according to the police the protests were pacific.

After this incident the paralysis began to manifest, a day after that occurred in Feu Rosa, now reaching Great Victory, Linhares, Aracruz, Colatina and Piúma. In this paralysis was alleged the reason why this happened as the compensation of the police, who received no increase for years. They also required aid and benefits such as food, and even receive more for Hazards and Health. New waistcoats were also requested because they were in great shortage and the police fleets were in very poor condition. After all the damage caused by the occurrence, the families were asking for anistía of the families and the policemen that could be imposed judicially against them. The situation was even more critical when civil police also stated that they would strike 2 days later because of the death of a Civil Police Officer who was acting in the place of a Military Police Officer.

On February 7, there were clashes between the civilians themselves. Groups of residents of the region went to the barracks to convince the demonstrators to vacate the place. Thus, residents protested demanding the return of policing. The army had to control the demonstration.

On February 10, representatives of the military police closed an agreement with the government to stop the movement. The negotiation ended without salary readjustment for the category, but it was agreed that the Government would give up the lawsuits against the associations, and form a commission to regulate the hours of the police. One of the demonstrators occupying the door of the Special Missions Battalion (BME), in Vitória, denied that the movement had ended after the meeting between associations and government. The following day, protesters ignored the agreement between the government and the associations, claiming the lack of legitimacy of the agreement.

On February 23, the State Court ordered that all persons blocking the Maruípe Barracks or the battalions of the Military Police be identified, summoned and summoned. The judge in charge authorized, in this case, the use of the police force. Those who did not leave the front of the battalions could be fined ten thousand reais a day. After a meeting with the State Government initiated on February 24, mediated by the Public Labor Ministry, there was consensus among the parties, so the women and family vacated the barracks on the morning of February 24. The first hearing to negotiate benefits was held on March 2.

On February 10, 2017, the Military Police of Espírito Santo indicted 703 military police officers for the crime of revolt, of which 327 the day before. If convicted, the penalty is 8 to 20 years of detention in military prison and the expulsion of the corporation. According to the Secretary of Public Security, André Garcia, these policemen had the cut off point and will not receive salary. The secretary reported that they were indicted for the military crime of revolt because they were armed and quartered in the battalions. Conduct of investigations will be carried out by the PM's Office.

On 21 February, the Military Police reported that 2,851 police officers were to respond to internal investigations for "risk to discipline" and "damage to society or corporation", about 28% of the force.

The court judge of the military audit has ordered the arrest of four police officers suspected of commencing the movement in the barracks. The four suspects are arrested, including the former federal and military deputy of the reserve Lucinio Castelo de Assumção, better known as Captain Assumção. On March 29, five military police officers who tried to stop the arrest of Captain Assumption on February 25 were detained. According to the Justice, they are accused of resistance, threat and aggressions.

Administrative proceedings of 280 police officers have been slowed down and will be released from deportation penalty, but may be sentenced to 30 days in prison. Another 40 military personnel continue to respond to administrative proceedings and may be expelled from the corporation


  1. Back to the streets

Of the 78 municipalities of Espírito Santo, 23 have the full force of the Military Police in the streets, according to data released by the Secretariat of State for Public Security (Sesp) until February 22. However, 71% of the cities in the state still follow with partial policing in the streets. The State has 9,500 PM military personnel, 6,500 of whom have returned to their activitie


  1. Consequences

According to the Espirito Santo Civil Police Union, 199 people have suffered violent deaths in Espírito Santo since the protests began until February 24. The most violent days were February 6, with 40 dead, and the next day with 22 deaths. The city with the highest number of deaths was the most populous state in the state, with 32 homicides until February 13. As a comparison, according to data from the Secretariat of State for Public Security, between February 4 and 10 of the previous year, 25 homicides occurred. Throughout February 2012, there were 158, and in February 2016.

According to the Brazilian Bar Association in the state, until February 10, 90% of the dead were men, with a concentration of victims between the ages of 17 and 20, and most were victims of gun fire Among those killed are users of illegal drugs, people with criminal records, victims of stray bullets and people with disabilities. Also killed were a civilian police officer and the president of the Guarapari Road Workers' Union.

With violence escalating steadily, the state government has called for help from the Armed Forces and the National Force to stop them.

More than a hundred bodies arrived at DML de Vitoria. The DML of Vitória got its refrigerators full and with bodies scattered along its corridors. Under normal conditions, three to four bodies are pericited and two are released per day in the DML of the capital, which has a maximum of 36

In addition to the deaths, more than 600 vehicles were stolen during this period, with 200 vehicles only in one day, and 300 stores were looted, estimating a loss of 300 million reais to trade. The number of occurrences increased tenfold compared to the mean in the state. According to the president of the Union of the Civil Police, this one does not have enough effective to investigate crimes of this magnitude

At the time of the strike, shops, schools and health posts were closed. The return to school was suspended for public school students. Some bank branches and shopping malls functioned regularly.

Due to the acts of violence in the State, on February 9 and 10, Vale, operator of the passenger train of the Vitória-Minas Railroad (EFVM), suspended the circulation of the composition in the stretch that passes through the Spirit Holy. In those two days, the train operated only between Belo Horizonte and Governador Valadares, in the eastern region of Minas Gerais.

By February 20, 28 municipalities of the state have announced the cancellation of Carnival programming. They alleged lack of security, since only part of the Military Police is in the streets.
