  1. What is the Public Safety?

Public safety is the state of normality that allows the usufruct of rights and the fulfillment of duties, its illegitimate alteration constituting a violation of basic rights, usually accompanied by violence, which produces events of insecurity and crime. It is a process, that is, a continuous sequence of facts or operations that present a certain unity or that reproduce with a certain regularity, that shares a vision focused on preventive, repressive, judicial, health and social components. It is a systemic process, due to the need to integrate a set of state knowledge and tools that should interact with the same vision, commitments and objectives.

It must also be optimized, since it depends on quick decisions, sanitation measures and results Immediate. Since public order is a state of serenity, appeasement and public tranquility, in keeping with the laws, precepts and customs that regulate coexistence in society, the preservation of this citizen's right will only be ample if the Concept of public safety is applied.


  1. The Federal Police

The Federal Police is a Brazilian police institution, subordinated to the Ministry of Justice, whose function, according to the 1988 Constitution, is to exercise public security for the preservation of public order and the safety of persons, as well as the assets and interests of the Union, carrying out maritime, airport and border police activities, repression of narcotics trafficking, smuggling and misconduct, and exercising exclusively the functions of the Union's judicial police.

The Federal Police, according to article 144, paragraph 1, of the Federal Constitution, is established by law as a permanent body, organized and maintained by the Union and structured in a career.

This, in addition to guarding national borders - preventing the entry of weapons, drugs or contraband - is responsible for investigating crimes that affect the country as a whole. For example, so-called "white collar" crimes (corruption), crimes against the financial system, etc.

The headquarters of the Federal Police is located in Brasília, in the Federal District, with decentralized units (regional superintendencies) in all the capitals of the states of the federation, as well as police stations and outposts in several cities of the country. Since January 14, 2011, the General Directorate has been held by delegate Leandro Daiello Coimbra.


  1. The Military Police

The Military Police have a duty to patrol the streets of cities, to inhibit and prevent the action of criminals, and when it can not avoid, try to arrest the thug after he has committed the crime. It is also the police that oversee the traffic, and may apply fines.

In Brazil, the State Military Police are the 27 public security forces whose function is the ostensive police and the preservation of public order, with exclusivity in ostensive police action, within the states (and the Federal District). Sub- Administratively to the governors and are, for purposes of organization, auxiliary and reserve forces of the Brazilian Army, and integrate the system of public security and social defense of Brazil, being subordinated to the State Secretaries of Security at operational level. They are borne by each member state and, in the case of the Federal District, by the Union.

Its members are called state military, as are the members of the military fire brigades, and are thus subordinate, when in service, to the state military court.


  1. The Civil Police

The Civil Police is headed by Police Officers, who coordinate the activities of the Agents. Each state has a different Civil Police, and they act investigating and elucidating crimes that occur within the territorial limits of the respective state, but can happen to fulfill missions outside, for example, arresting a criminal who committed a crime in Rio, but went to hide In Goiás. In this case, the Rio Civil Police must inform the Civil Police of Goiás that it is carrying out a mission in its territory.

As regards their duties, the Civil Police has several functions. It is responsible for overseeing various activities, such as authorizing major events and operating certain commercial activities. But the most important activity is undoubtedly the investigation and elucidation of crimes committed in its territory.


  1. The Municipal Guard

The Municipal Guard (GM) is the denomination used in Brazil to designate the institutions that can be created by the municipalities to collaborate in public security [1] [2], using the police power delegated by the municipality through complementary laws. Some local administrations have used the denomination Municipal Civil Guard to designate the organ in cities of the interior and Civil Guard Metropolitan for the great capitals of Brazil.

The Municipal Guard collaborates in public safety by using police power and city council delegates through complementary laws. Some local administrations have used the name Municipal Civil Guard to designate an organ in cities of the interior and Metropolitan Civil Guard for the great capitals of Brazil.


  1. The Brazilian Army

The Brazilian Army (EB) is one of the three Armed Forces of Brazil, responsible, externally, for defending the country in eminently terrestrial operations and, internally, for the guarantee of law, order and constitutional powers.

The Supreme Commander is the President of the Republic.

The Army is framed in the Ministry of Defense, alongside the Navy and the Air Force, since 1999 within the structure of the Government of Brazil. At the top of the organization is the Supreme Commander, a civilian post exercised by the President of the Republic. Below him is the Army Commander. Following are the largest organizational levels in the heads of the general, sectoral and operational management bodies.


  1. The Brazilian Navy

Navy of Brazil (MB) is the branch of the Armed Forces of Brazil responsible for conducting naval operations. The Brazilian Navy is the largest in South America and Latin America, as well as being the second largest navy in America, after the United States Navy. Its patron is the Marquis of Tamandaré.

The primary mission of the Navy is to guarantee the defense of the Homeland together with the other Armed Forces. In order to fulfill its constitutional mission, the Navy must prepare and apply the Naval Power. The Navy, as a secondary mission, is also responsible for cooperating with national development and civil defense, as determined by the President of the Republic.


  1. The Brazilian Air Force

Brazilian Air Force (FAB) is the aerial branch of the Brazilian Armed Forces and one of three national uniformed services. The FAB was formed when the Army and Navy airborne branches were merged into a single military force. Both branches of air transferred their equipment, facilities and personnel to the new armed force.

The military command of the force is exercised by the COMAER Aeronautics Command, to which are subordinated three Commands-General, three departments and several other organs related to the operation and administration of Brazilian aviation, both civil and military, and Aerospace research and development .
